Buchty

History

The history of buchty goes back to the 17th century, when people disovered growing fermenting yeast. Buchty were just plain dough balls that were cooked in hot water and served with melted butter on top. It was a simple treat that even the poorer classes could enjoy. They were served at various important occasions like Christmas however they could also be served on the most ordinary day.


Description

Buchty are small dough cubes that are be filled with a jam or a filling of your choice. They have a very good taste and are very sweet. Most people add sugar on top to make it even more sweet.

Ingredients

  • 400g Flour
  • 1 cube yeast
  • 50g sugar
  • 200 ml milk
  • a little salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 100g butter

Recipe

  1. Heat the milk so that it is lukewarm but not hot. Pour 1/2 cup milk into a cup, add a teaspoon of sugar, add active dry yeast, and stir. Leave it in a warm place until small bubbles form on the surface of the activated yeast. This takes about 10-15 minutes.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, place the flour, sugar, the rest of the lukewarm milk, melted butter, a pinch of salt, and an egg. Add milk with activated yeast.
  3. Start stirring the ingredients with a fork a wooden spoon; when combined, dump the mass on the work surface. Knead the dough properly. It requires a bit of physical effort to make the dough smooth and slightly sticky. It takes about 10 minutes
  4. Let the dough rise in a warm place until doubled in size.
  5. Divide the dough into approximately equal-sized pieces. If you have a kitchen scale, weigh each piece of dough per bun to 3 ounces (85 grams). Roll each piece with a rolling pin into a patty.
  6. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling and seal well. Finally, gently roll the finished bun between your palms to get a regular shape.
  7. Grease a baking dish with butter. Melt 1/3 stick of butter in a saucepan and set aside to cool. Brush the baking tray with it.
  8. Place the prepared buns one at a time, seam-side down, in the baking dish, brushing each side with melted but not hot butter.
  9. Brush the buchty bun tops with the remaining butter.
